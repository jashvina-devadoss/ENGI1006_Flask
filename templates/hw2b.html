Homework 2B: Simulations

In Homework 2B, we simplified a real world problem with a model. We wrote a module with a series of functions, then imported the module into a tester script to execute and test the program.

Our aim was to simulate the position of a projectile launched straight up into the air, taking into account gravity’s variation with altitude.

We translated a series of equations into a sequence of Python functions, using intermediate outputs to iteratively compute the position after each time step, assuming that velocity is constant at each time step. Python tips: When simultaneous updating of variables is required (e.g. in an iteration of a loop), a useful method is to assign tuples of equations to tuples of variables (e.g. v1, v2 = function_1(v1, v2), function_2(v1, v2) ). I also encountered a rounding error while looping that affected the results— the nature of the problem was that the time step was small enough such that at (for example) 2.99, the loop continued, updating to a new value for velocity, instead of rounding to 3 seconds, resulting in a large leap in values between 2 to 3 seconds.

We plotted the simulation results, comparing them to the calculated position when acceleration due to gravity is assumed to be constant. We showed that since gravity decreases with altitude, the object is shown to travel further away from Earth when the variation of gravity with altitude is accounted for.

We saw that there were tradeoffs when it came to errors in the simulation. As the time step decreased, the simulation accuracy increased, while the simulation accuracy decreased as the time step increased. The longer the simulation ran, the more errors there were.
